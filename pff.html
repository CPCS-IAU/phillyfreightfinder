These businesses can be grouped by industrial sectors to better explain the type of economic activity in which each establishment is involved.

When considering each of these sectors and their relationship with freight transportation it is helpful to associate the industry sector with a component of the supply with which it is involved.

We can generally break a supply chain in to four distinct components.

Extraction

These industries are involved in the extraction of raw materials from the earth and/or the raising of animals and crops.

Production

These industries are the makers. They are responsible for the production of the goods that allow for our way of life. Their operations have a critical reliance on freight transportation system to deliver the necessary inputs and get their outputs, or final products to market.

Distribution

These industries serve as the vital link between the other components of the supply chain. Their business is the conveyance, storage, and xXxX of freight.
